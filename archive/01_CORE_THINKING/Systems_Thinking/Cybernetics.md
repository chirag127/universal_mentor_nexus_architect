# Cybernetics

## Summary

Cybernetics is the scientific study of control and communication in living organisms, machines, and organizations. It explores how systems, whether biological, mechanical, or social, regulate themselves through feedback loops to maintain stability, achieve goals, and adapt to changing environments. Key concepts include: **feedback** (positive and negative), **homeostasis** (self-regulation), **information theory**, **systems thinking**, and **emergent properties**. Cybernetics provides a framework for understanding complex interactions and designing intelligent, adaptive systems.
