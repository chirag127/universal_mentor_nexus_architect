# Stock and Flow Diagrams

## SUMMARY

Stock and Flow Diagrams (SFDs) are a powerful visual modeling tool used in system dynamics to represent the behavior of complex systems over time. They consist of two primary elements: **stocks**, which represent accumulations or quantities that change over time (e.g., inventory, cash, knowledge); and **flows**, which represent the rates at which stocks change (e.g., production rate, sales rate, learning rate). Stocks are depicted as rectangular boxes, and flows as pipes with valves, indicating the direction and magnitude of movement into or out of a stock. Auxiliary variables and connectors are used to define relationships and feedback loops within the system. SFDs enable the simulation of system dynamics and the identification of leverage points for intervention.
